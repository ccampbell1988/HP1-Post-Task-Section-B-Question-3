Team Selection & Tryout Process

The player evaluation & selection process is extremely important and creates your base for success for the season. Below you will find the various tools and strategies to leverage to help in these 2 areas.

1

Phase 1: Pre-Tryout Identification & Preparation

This foundational phase occurs before any player steps on the ice. It involves:

  • Scouting & Information Gathering: For higher levels, this involves scouting previous season's games, and gathering information from previous coaches (respecting privacy and ethical guidelines).
  • Defining Team Needs & Identity: Clearly outline the desired player attributes based on the "Team Canada Identity" (e.g., Creative & Dynamic, High Compete Level, Manages Pucks, Hard to Play Against) and the specific needs of the team for the upcoming season.
  • Establishing Evaluation Criteria: Develop a clear, objective set of criteria based on the four pillars (Technical, Tactical, Physical, Mental) and the team's identity. This should align with Hockey Canada's skill assessment guidelines.
  • Staff Preparation: Ensure all evaluators understand the criteria, their roles, and the evaluation tools to be used. Consistency in evaluation is key.
2

Phase 2: Initial On-Ice Evaluation (Camp Days 1-2)

The first two days of camp focus on assessing core abilities:

  • Skill-Based Drills: Utilize drills from Hockey Canada's Player Assessment plans (e.g., Skating Agility Combinations, Puck Control Drills, Shooting Sequences, 1v1s). Focus on assessing fundamental skills: skating, puck handling, passing, shooting, and defensive skills. Standardized drill sheets with clear evaluation points are used.
  • Small Area Games (SAGs): Excellent for evaluating hockey sense, decision-making under pressure, competitiveness, and skill application in tight spaces. Observe "Puck Pursuit," "Winning Battles and Races," and "Managing Pucks." Observation checklists are key.
  • Initial Goaltender Evaluation: Specific drills focusing on positioning, rebound control, save selection, and puck handling.
3

Phase 3: Game-Situation Evaluation (Camp Days 2-3)

This is a crucial phase where tactical understanding and game application are assessed:

  • Scrimmages/Exhibition Games: Assess tactical understanding, game application of skills, hockey IQ, and performance under game-like pressure. Evaluate "Hockey Sense" (General, Defensive, Offensive Awareness) and "Team Canada Identity" traits. Game evaluation forms and video recording are used.
  • Bench Management Observation: How players interact on the bench, respond to coaching, and support teammates.
4

Phase 4: Off-Ice Evaluation & Interviews

Evaluation extends beyond the rink, integrated throughout and post-camp:

  • Physical Testing: (As appropriate for age/level) Tests for speed, agility, power, endurance (sprints, jumps, beep test). Standardized recording sheets used.
  • Mental Performance Assessment: Observe resilience, response to adversity, focus, and coachability. Consider short questionnaires or informal interviews.
  • Character & Leadership Assessment: Observe interactions, respect, and leadership potential. Coach observations and interview questions are key.
  • Player Interviews (shortlisted candidates): Discuss goals, understanding of team expectations, and coachability.
5

Phase 5: Final Evaluation & Roster Selection

The culmination of the tryout process:

  • Evaluation Meetings: Coaching staff and evaluators meet to discuss all collected data, comparing notes and ranking players.
  • Consideration of Team Composition: Balance of positions, player roles, and overall team chemistry.
  • Difficult Decisions: Acknowledge that some cuts will be tough, focusing on the overall best fit for the team's goals.
  • Player Feedback: Provide constructive feedback to all players, especially those not selected, offering guidance for future development (crucial for LTPD).

Evaluation Tools

A combination of subjective (observational) and objective (measurable) tools are employed to create a well-rounded profile of each athlete. Tools are chosen based on the age and competitive level of the group, ensuring a multifaceted evaluation.

🏒 On-Ice Tools

  • Hockey Canada Player Assessment Drills & Plans: Standardized drills for assessing specific technical skills (skating, puck control, shooting, passing, defense) and basic tactical elements. Tracked with evaluation sheets (e.g., 1-4 rating scale).
  • Small Area Games (SAGs): Evaluate skill application, decision-making, competitiveness, and hockey sense in confined, game-like situations. Tracked via observational notes and video.
  • Scrimmages/Exhibition Games: Assess overall game performance, tactical understanding, consistency, and response to game pressure. Tracked with individual player forms, video analysis (Hudl, Sportscode), and basic/advanced stats.
  • Goaltender-Specific Drills: Evaluate save selection, rebound control, positioning, tracking, and puck handling. Tracked via goalie coach observations and video.

🏋️ Off-Ice Tools

  • Physical Fitness Testing: Measure aerobic/anaerobic capacity, power, strength, agility (e.g., Beep Test, sprints, jumps). Tracked with standardized recording sheets. Professional teams use force plates, laser timing.
  • Mental Performance & Psychological Assessments: Gauge mental toughness, coachability, motivation via interviews, questionnaires (e.g., ACSI, TOPS at elite levels), and behavioral observation.
  • Character & Leadership Evaluation: Assess teamwork, communication, respect via coach/peer observations, references, and scenario-based interview questions.
  • Academic Performance (Student-Athletes): Ensure eligibility and assess responsibility. Tracked via school transcripts.
  • Medical History & Physical Examination: Ensure health and identify pre-existing conditions. Tracked via medical clearance forms.
  • Advanced Tools: Advanced video platforms (XOS, Catapult), player tracking (NHL EDGE), biometric wearables (Firstbeat, Whoop), sophisticated scouting databases, psychometric testing services.

In-Season Evaluation Plan

In-season evaluation is an ongoing process crucial for player development, providing feedback, and making informed coaching decisions. It should be integrated into the daily, weekly, and monthly team rhythm across four key pillars.

Technical Pillar (Skill Execution & Refinement)

Focus: Assessing the quality and consistency of fundamental skills (skating, puck skills, passing, shooting, checking) in practice and game situations. Identifying areas for improvement based on Hockey Canada's "Skill Deficiencies" and "Skill Strengths."

Methods:
  • Practice Observation: Assess drill performance, repetition quality (deliberate practice), provide immediate, specific feedback.
  • Video Analysis (Individual Clips): Show players clips of their technique, compare to models or past bests, use telestration.
  • Skill-Specific Testing/Challenges: Occasional focused assessment of progress (e.g., shooting accuracy, skating times).

Frequency: Daily (practice observation), Weekly (video review sessions).

Tactical Pillar (Hockey Sense & System Play)

Focus: Assessing decision-making, understanding and execution of team systems (breakouts, D-zone coverage), positional play, and overall hockey IQ, including "Athlete Expectations" (General, Defensive, Offensive Awareness).

Methods:
  • Game Observation & Analysis: Live tracking with "Game Card," video review (team, positional, individual) to discuss system execution and "Canadian Way Principles."
  • Practice Scenarios & SAGs: Replicate game situations to assess tactical understanding.
  • "Chalk Talks" / Whiteboard Sessions: Discuss concepts and allow players to demonstrate understanding.

Frequency: Post-game (video review), Weekly (tactical focus in practices and meetings).

Physical Pillar (Conditioning, Strength, Durability)

Focus: Monitoring fitness levels, recovery, injury prevention, ensuring players meet physical demands. Links to "S&C HP1 Coaching Presentation."

Methods:
  • Observation in Practice & Games: Energy levels, pace maintenance, physicality.
  • Communication with S&C Coach: Regular updates on team's physical state, training load.
  • Periodic Fitness Re-testing: Mid-season, pre-playoffs to track progress.
  • Monitoring Training Load: Using RPE or tools to prevent overtraining.
  • Injury Tracking & Management: Close work with athletic therapists.

Frequency: Daily (observation), Weekly (S&C sessions, S&C coach communication), Periodically (fitness testing).

Mental Pillar (Mindset, Focus, Resilience)

Focus: Assessing psychological skills: confidence, concentration, emotional control, resilience, motivation, coachability. Crucial for "Gen Z athletes."

Methods:
  • Behavioral Observation: Response to mistakes/pressure, effort, body language, coachability.
  • Individual Meetings & Check-ins: Regular conversations to understand players' mental state and challenges, building "Connection."
  • Goal Setting & Review: Work with players on SMART goals and review progress (process, performance, outcome).
  • Encouraging Mental Skills Practice: Introduce visualization, positive self-talk, "mental anchor" breathing.
  • Creating a Supportive Environment: Foster a culture of trust and psychological safety.

Frequency: Daily (observation, informal check-ins), Weekly/Bi-weekly (individual meetings).

In-Season Progress Tracking

Tracking progress is essential to ensure development, provide players with tangible evidence of growth, and adjust training plans. The Self-Evaluation Radar is a powerful tool for players and coaches to visualize progress.

Key Tracking Tool Categories

Technical Tools

Video analysis (side-by-side technique comparison), skill-specific charts/checklists (based on Hockey Canada assessment points, re-assessed monthly), and drill performance metrics (e.g., agility skating times, shooting accuracy percentages).

Tactical Tools

Game statistics (basic: G, A, +/; advanced via video: zone entries/exits, pass completion %, TO/TA), video review with tagging of tactical decisions/system execution, positional feedback forms, player self-reflection worksheets on tactical understanding.

Physical Tools

Fitness testing results (baseline and periodic re-tests), strength & conditioning logs (workout completion, weights lifted), wellness monitoring (daily questionnaires on sleep, soreness, mood), and athletic therapist injury logs.

Mental Tools

Player journals/self-reflection prompts (goals, challenges, mental state), SMART goal worksheets with periodic reviews, coach's behavioral observation notes (response to adversity, feedback consistency), and anonymous team cohesion surveys.
